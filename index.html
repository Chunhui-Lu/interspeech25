Audio samples from "Robust neural codec language modeling with phoneme position prediction for zero-shot TTS"

Authors: Chunhui Lu, Xue Wen, Liming Song, Junkwang Oh

Abstract:
Though large language models (LLMs) based methods have achieved remarkable progress in zero-shot Text-to-Speech (TTS) synthesis, they suffer from robustness issues including mispronunciation, word skipping, and word repeating. To address these robustness challenges, we propose incorporating phoneme position prediction into the LLM-based TTS model. More concretely, given an input phoneme sequence as condition, our model autoregressively predicts acoustic codes and their corresponding phoneme positions within the input sequence synchronously. This mechanism ensures accurate and complete alignment between acoustic codes and phonemes. Experimental results demonstrate that our system significantly reduces phoneme skipping/repetition errors compared to strong baselines, achieving a 52.7% relative reduction in character error rate while maintaining comparable performance in zero-shot TTS evaluations.
 

This pages contains a set of speech samples used in our evaluations.All utterances and speakers are unseen in model training.

 

Compared systems

    GT: ground-truth audio reconstructed by the UniAudio's GT model.
    VALL-E: Implemented exactly following VALL-E, without incorporating any robustness improving techniques.
    VALL-E R: Following VALL-E R, the system predicts acoustic codes and their corresponding phoneme identities synchronously and uses its decoding strategy.
    VALL-E R (Greedy): The same system as VALL-E R, but using greedy decoding.
    VALL-E BOTH: Based on VALL-E, the system combines phoneme identity and position prediction.
    OURS: Our proposed method.
 

Wilcoxon signed-rank tests result

Due to limited paper space, we attached the p-values of Wilcoxon signed-rank tests for all evaluations here.

    GT: p-values between our system and all baselines are smaller than 1e-5.
    CMOS: OURS vs GT (0.152), VALL-E vs GT (0.729), VALL-E R vs GT (0.925).
    SMOS: OURS vs VALL-E (0.074), OURS vs VALL-E R (0.133).
These results demonstrate statistically significant superiority of our system in robustness (α=0.05), aligning with our core technical contributions.
